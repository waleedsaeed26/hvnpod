# HVNPOD Brand Guidelines

**Version 1.0 — March 2026**

## 1. Brand Overview

HVNPOD is a premium design-led brand creating acoustic privacy pods for modern workspaces.

We start with pods — and we scale. The roadmap moves from acoustic pods into smart furniture, modular space systems, and ultimately future living environments. Every product category we enter will carry the same design DNA: architectural, considered, and built for people who treat their environment as a reflection of how they think.

The name is deliberate. A haven. A pod. Compressed, typeset in caps, with no vowels in the first word — because brevity and precision are core to what we make.

### Mission

To give people ownership of their acoustic environment — without construction, without compromise.

### Vision

A world where privacy is designed in, not retrofitted. Where every workspace, home, and shared environment has dedicated space for focus, conversation, and calm.

### Where We Start, Where We Go

| Phase | Category | Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| Now | Acoustic privacy pods | Launch |
| Next | Smart furniture (desks, chairs, panels) | 12–24 months |
| Then | Modular space systems | 24–48 months |
| Future | Living and working environments | Long-term |

## 2. Brand Positioning

### The Core Idea

**Premium acoustic privacy, deployed fast, without construction.**

HVNPOD is not a renovation. It is not a phone booth. It is not furniture from a catalog. It is a precision-designed acoustic environment — a room within a room — that arrives ready, installs in days, and transforms how a space functions.

### Who We Serve

| Segment | Profile | Pain Point |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate HR & Facilities | Companies with 50–500+ employees in open-plan offices | No private space for calls, focus work, sensitive conversations |
| Coworking Spaces | Operators competing on amenity quality | Tenants need acoustic privacy; build-out is not possible |
| Architecture & Interior Firms | Specifiers selecting product for client fit-outs | Need a pod that matches the design language of premium spaces |
| Design-Conscious Decision Makers | Individual purchasers or managers with discretionary budget | Refuse to compromise on aesthetics; value product as statement |

### What We Are

- A considered design object that happens to be a workspace
- An acoustic solution that respects the space it enters
- A premium product with a clear value proposition: **silence, on demand**
- Deployable in days, not months

### What We Are Not

Positioning is as much about refusal as it is about claim. HVNPOD does not compete on:

- Price (we are not cheap, and we do not position against cheap alternatives)
- Office-only use cases (pods belong in studios, homes, hotels, clinics)
- Technology novelty (we are not a gadget — features serve acoustic function, not the reverse)

## 3. Design Principles

These four principles govern every design decision — product, digital, print, spatial, and communication.

### 3.1 Architectural Minimalism

Every element earns its place. If a surface, line, or detail does not serve a structural, acoustic, or user-experience function, it is removed. Our products look like they were designed by someone who respected the viewer's intelligence enough not to overexplain.

**Applied to:** Product form, website layout, packaging, marketing materials.

### 3.2 Tactile Premium

Materials and finishes communicate quality through touch and sight before a single word is read. We choose surfaces that age well, feel substantial, and reward close inspection. The seams are straight. The weight is right. The finish does not apologize.

**Applied to:** Pod interior and exterior materials, hardware choices, printed collateral, digital texture use.

### 3.3 Functional Elegance

Beauty that serves a purpose — never decorative for its own sake. The acoustic panel is beautiful because it performs. The ventilation grille is precise because it must be. Ornament that does not serve function is eliminated.

**Applied to:** Product detailing, UI/UX, information hierarchy in all communications.

### 3.4 Confident Restraint

Knowing what to leave out is as important as what to include. HVNPOD does not shout. It does not use ten words when two will do. It does not decorate when clean space communicates better. The brand holds silence as seriously as it holds substance.

**Applied to:** Copywriting, visual composition, color usage, product feature set.

### 4.2 Primary Accent

| Token | Name | Hex | Usage Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| `hvn-forest` | Deep Forest | `#1B3D2F` | **PRIMARY ACCENT — 2–4% of any composition only** |
| `hvn-forest-light` | Forest Light | `#264D3B` | Forest hover states |

**Forest is the only color that carries brand intent.** It is used exclusively for:

- Call-to-action buttons
- Active navigation indicators
- Badges (availability, tier markers)
- Success states
- Accent lines in headings (rare, deliberate)

**Forest is never:**

- Used as a background for large sections
- Applied to decorative elements
- Combined with other accent colors
- Used where a neutral would serve equally well

**The Rule:** If Forest appears on a page and you are not immediately drawn to it as a focal point, it has been overused. If it does not appear at all, something functional is probably missing a clear visual anchor.

### 4.3 Usage Ratios

## 5. Typography

Typography is chosen for precision, readability, and architectural alignment. Three typefaces cover every use case.

### 5.1 Typeface Stack

| Role | Typeface | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Headings & Display | **Outfit** | Geometric, modern, bold — matches the pod's clean angular lines |
| Body & UI | **Inter** | Readable, professional, web-native, neutral at scale |
| Technical / Specs | **JetBrains Mono** | Connects to the technical and design-engineering audience; anchors specification data |

### 5.2 Scale & Usage Rules

| Level | Typeface | Weight | Size (desktop) | Tracking | Line Height |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Hero | Outfit | 700 Bold | 64–96px | -0.03em | 1.1 |
| H1 | Outfit | 700 Bold | 48–64px | -0.02em | 1.2 |
| H2 | Outfit | 600 SemiBold | 36–48px | -0.02em | 1.2 |
| H3 | Outfit | 600 SemiBold | 24–32px | -0.01em | 1.25 |
| H4 | Outfit | 500 Medium | 20–24px | 0 | 1.3 |
| Body (large) | Inter | 400 Regular | 18px | 0 | 1.6 |
| Body (standard) | Inter | 400 Regular | 16px | 0 | 1.6 |
| Caption / Label | Inter | 400 Regular | 12–14px | 0.02em | 1.5 |
| Spec / Data | JetBrains Mono | 400 Regular | 13–15px | 0 | 1.5 |

### 5.3 Type Rules

- **Never use decorative fonts.** Outfit, Inter, and JetBrains Mono are the complete stack.
- **Headings are tight.** Tracking at -0.02em by default for H1/H2.
- **Body text breathes.** Line-height of 1.6 minimum for all reading copy.
- **All-caps is permitted** for labels, badges, and navigation items — never for body paragraphs.
- **Spec callouts** use JetBrains Mono to signal precision: `30dB noise reduction`, `1.2m²`, `EGP 85,000`.

## 6. Logo Usage

### 6.1 Wordmark

**"HVNPOD"** set in Outfit Bold, with wide character tracking (+0.08em to +0.12em). The wordmark is always uppercase. No lowercase variants exist.

Color variants:
- On dark: `hvn-white` (`#FAFAF8`)
- On light: `hvn-black` (`#0A0A0A`)
- Accent (rare, specific applications): `hvn-forest` (`#1B3D2F`)

### 6.2 Symbol

The HVNPOD symbol is a set of concentric squares — precise, geometric, and nested. It represents:

- The pod within the space
- Personal boundaries defined by design
- Precision of line and intent
- A room within a room

The symbol may appear independently at scales where the wordmark would be illegible (favicon, app icon, embossed hardware), but should never replace the full wordmark in primary brand applications.

### 6.3 Clearspace

Minimum clearspace on all sides of the logo (wordmark or symbol): **1× the height of the cap-height of the wordmark.**

No text, graphic element, photography, or edge of a surface may enter this clearspace.

### 6.4 Logo Don'ts

- Do not distort, stretch, or compress the logo
- Do not change the logo color to anything outside the approved variants
- Do not add drop shadows, glows, or any visual effects
- Do not place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a clear background block
- Do not recreate the logo in a different typeface
- Do not animate the logo in ways that alter its form (subtle fade-in is acceptable)
- Do not use the wordmark at sizes below 80px wide in digital or 15mm wide in print

## 7. Tone of Voice

### 7.1 The Brand Voice in Three Words

**Quiet. Confident. Precise.**

HVNPOD does not perform enthusiasm. It does not over-explain. It does not hedge. It states what is true, clearly, and trusts the reader to understand.

### 7.2 Voice Principles

**Quiet:** The brand does not shout. Silence is a feature of the product — it is also a feature of how we communicate. White space on a page is not empty; it is considered.

**Confident:** We do not say "we think" or "we believe" unless we are expressing genuine opinion. We say what is. "30dB of quiet." Not "up to 30dB of potential noise reduction in optimal conditions."

**Precise:** Every word is chosen. We do not reach for adjectives when a noun or verb will do the work. We do not use filler phrases.

### 7.3 What We Never Do

| Avoid | Because |
|---|---|
| Exclamation marks in brand copy | They undermine confidence |
| "Amazing", "innovative", "revolutionary" | Hollow adjectives that every brand uses |
| "Transformative", "game-changing" | Overused, unearned, vague |
| Passive voice where active is possible | It distances the brand from what it makes |
| Overly long sentences with multiple clauses that accumulate meaning until the reader has lost the thread | Brevity is a form of respect |

### 7.4 Preferred Vocabulary

**Words that belong in HVNPOD copy:**

`precise` · `considered` · `designed` · `engineered` · `acoustic` · `privacy` · `focus` · `calm` · `quiet` · `installed` · `deployed` · `contained` · `measured` · `refined`

**Words to use sparingly (earn them):**

`premium` · `performance` · `elevated` · `curated`

**Words never used:**

`amazing` · `innovative` · `game-changing` · `revolutionary` · `cutting-edge` · `state-of-the-art` · `seamless` (unless describing a literal seam)

### 7.5 Voice Examples

| Context | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | "Transform your workspace with our amazing acoustic pods!" | "30dB of quiet. Installed in days." |
| Product description | "The HVNPOD One is an innovative single-person pod that will revolutionize how you work." | "The HVNPOD One. Designed for solo focus, deep work, and calls that require concentration." |
| CTA | "Click here to get started on your journey!" | "Book a trial" |
| Value proposition | "Our state-of-the-art pods are changing the game in workplace acoustic solutions." | "Acoustic privacy without construction. Deployed in 48 hours." |
| Spec callout | "Up to an amazing 30dB of noise reduction!" | `30dB noise reduction` |
| Social caption | "Can't believe how beautiful our new pod looks! So excited!! 🔥🔥" | "HVNPOD One. Charcoal finish. Available now." |

### 7.6 Long-Form Tone

For case studies, articles, and LinkedIn content: the tone shifts slightly toward authoritative and data-backed, but never becomes corporate. Write like a thoughtful founder or a design-aware facilities director — not like a press release.

## 8. Photography Direction

### 8.1 Studio Renders

Primary product imagery is rendered against architectural backgrounds: raw concrete, poured resin, dark stone, industrial steel. The pod is the hero. The environment is in service of the product.

**Composition rules:**
- Pods shown at slight angle (not flat-on) to communicate three-dimensionality
- Foreground and background elements minimal — no distracting props
- Natural-feeling light from one strong directional source, supplemented by ambient fill
- Deep space behind the pod — the pod does not appear crowded

### 8.2 Contextual Photography

When pods are shown in real environments (installed in offices, coworking spaces):

- Shoot during off-hours or with minimal human presence to keep the product as visual anchor
- Environment should be premium — no cluttered, low-quality spaces
- Framing: wide establishing shot, then close-in details
- Avoid showing branded items from other companies in frame

### 8.3 People in Photography

People are shown when they demonstrate the product's function — not to demonstrate lifestyle aspiration or staged happiness.

**Do:**
- Show someone inside a pod, focused on a screen, clearly in productive thought
- Show the pod door closing — motion, purpose
- Show hands on the pod surface — communicating materiality

**Do not:**
- Show people smiling at camera
- Show staged group interactions around a pod (it is a single or small-group product)
- Use stock photography aesthetics — perfect lighting, perfect clothes, perfect expressions

### 8.4 Color Grading

- Warm neutrals dominant (slight amber push in shadows)
- Slight desaturation — not cold, not clinical
- Blacks are deep and clean — no lifted shadows unless intentional
- Forest accent color in the product must read true: `#1B3D2F`, not shifted to blue or yellow-green

### 8.5 Product Colors in Photography

Each pod color variant should have hero imagery:

| Color Variant | Background Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Cream | Dark concrete, charcoal wall surface |
| Charcoal | Warm light industrial background, raw timber |
| Forest | Neutral concrete or plaster — the color speaks for itself |
| Blue | Clean architectural background, poured grey |

## 10. What HVNPOD Is Not

Clarity of positioning requires clarity of refusal. These statements serve as guardrails for all brand communications.

| Misconception | The Truth |
|---|---|
| "It's a phone booth" | It is an architectural acoustic environment. A phone booth is disposable infrastructure. HVNPOD is designed. |
| "It's a cheap solution" | It is a premium product. The price reflects engineering, materials, and acoustic performance. There is no budget tier. |
| "It's just for open offices" | It belongs in coworking spaces, studios, clinics, hotels, homes, and any environment where acoustic privacy matters. |
| "It's a permanent construction" | It is deployed, not built. It can be relocated, reconfigured, or removed. No construction permits required. |
| "It's a tech gadget" | Technology serves acoustic function. HVNPOD is not defined by its features — it is defined by what it does to your environment. |
